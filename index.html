Ramana Maharshi

The Silent Sage of Arunachala

Indian Hindu sage and spiritual teacher

About Ramana Maharshi

Ramana Maharshi was an Indian Hindu sage and jivanmukta (liberated being). He was born as Venkataraman Iyer on December 30, 1879, in Tiruchuzhi, Tamil Nadu, India.

At the age of 16, he experienced a profound spiritual awakening that led him to renounce worldly life and move to the sacred hill of Arunachala in Tiruvannamalai. There, he spent the rest of his life.

Ramana Maharshi advocated the path of self-inquiry, which he called "Atma Vichara." He emphasized the importance of seeking the true self and realizing one's identity with the absolute. Throughout his life, he encouraged people to turn inward and seek the source of their consciousness. His teachings continue to inspire spiritual seekers around the world.

He believed in the unity of all things and the illusory nature of death and evil. Ramana Maharshi's teachings have been widely disseminated, and his legacy continues to inspire spiritual seekers globally.

Teachings of Ramana Maharshi

Ramana Maharshi's teachings primarily focus on the practice of self-inquiry, which he referred to as "Atma Vichara". He taught that by repeatedly asking oneself "Who am I?", one could realize their true self, which is the absolute consciousness beyond the ego and physical body.

He emphasized the importance of self-realization and the understanding that the self is the ultimate reality. According to him, the mind is the source of all suffering, and by turning inward, one can dissolve the mind and experience true peace and bliss.

Ramana Maharshi also taught the concept of non-duality (Advaita), which asserts that there is no separation between the individual self and the absolute. He believed that everything in the universe is interconnected and that realizing this unity is the key to liberation.

His teaching of self-inquiry is not just a meditation technique but a way of living. By staying in the presence of the "I", the true self, one begins to dissolve the false sense of individuality and recognize the oneness of existence.

Legacy of Ramana Maharshi

Ramana Maharshi's influence extends far beyond his physical presence. He inspired countless spiritual seekers, scholars, and ordinary people to explore the depths of their own consciousness and seek self-realization.

His ashram in Tiruvannamalai continues to be a place of pilgrimage for those seeking spiritual guidance and inner peace. The teachings of Ramana Maharshi have been translated into numerous languages and continue to be studied and revered by people around the world.

Today, his teachings are preserved through books, recordings, and the efforts of dedicated followers who spread his message of self-inquiry and self-realization. Ramana Maharshi remains a beacon of light for all those seeking the truth beyond the transient world.
